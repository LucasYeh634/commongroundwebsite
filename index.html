The story

From performance to purpose

01

Music started as a discipline.

I grew up around music, moving from piano to violin at a young age. At first, the path was familiar: practice, recitals, and competitions. Over time, I began to understand that music mattered most when it became something shared.

02

Then the world became quiet.

During the pandemic, my brother and I played from our balcony and for people who were isolated at home. The music was only the beginning. What stayed with me were the faces, stories, and conversations that followed.

03

Connection moved into medicine.

We continued performing for medical groups, celebrations, funerals, hospitals, and retirement communities. These experiences showed me that people do not always need another feed. Sometimes they need a reason to gather, listen, and feel seen.

04

Listening became as important as performing.

At senior communities, the generational gap first felt intimidating. After the final note, however, the real exchange began. A song could open a memory. A memory could begin a relationship.

The idea

Design for depth, not just attention.

CommonGround is not a finished platform. It is a question I want to explore: can technology help people find community without turning every interaction into content?

01

Intentional communities

Smaller spaces organized around shared interests, service, experiences, and local life.

02

Thoughtful prompts

Questions that invite stories and reflection rather than performance and comparison.

03

Meaningful introductions

Connections based on what people can learn from one another—not follower counts.

The next step

Turn a lived experience into a working prototype.

At Hack the North, I hope to learn from developers, designers, and builders who can challenge the idea and help transform it into something people can test.
