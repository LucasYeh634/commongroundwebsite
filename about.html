About

From music to community

My music career began when I was two, influenced by my mom’s full-time piano teaching. I started with piano and switched to violin at age three. Over time, practice, recitals, and competitions became familiar, and I eventually performed at Carnegie Hall.

When the pandemic shut down public life, my brother and I began performing Bach’s Concerto for Two Violins from our balcony. Neighbors listened from the sidewalk, six feet apart.

After restrictions lifted, we continued performing for teachers, anniversaries, funerals, senior homes, hospitals, and community events. The most memorable part was not the applause. It was the conversations afterward.

At Case Western Reserve University, I joined Musicians in Medicine and later became Outreach Coordinator. These experiences inspired me to explore how technology might strengthen—not replace—real human interaction.
